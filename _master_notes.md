## Key direct quotations gathered (will be cited verbatim)

> "We built a Software Factory: non-interactive development where specs + scenarios drive agents that write code, run harnesses, and converge without human review." — StrongDM team, via Willison

> "Code must not be written by humans. Code must not be reviewed by humans. If you haven't spent at least $1,000 on tokens today per human engineer, your software factory has room for improvement." — StrongDM cardinal rules

> "Why am I doing this?" — StrongDM mantra

> "Seed → Validation harness → Feedback loop. Tokens are the fuel." — StrongDM principles

> "How can you prove that software you are producing works if both the implementation and the tests are being written for you by coding agents?" — Willison

> "If an access management system fails because an agent-written module contained a subtle error that no human ever saw, who is liable? Three gaps stand out: nobody knows who is liable, nobody knows what to disclose, and the contracts have not caught up." — Stanford Law CodeX

> "The harness and models matter, but the prompts matter more." — Cursor scaling-agents post

> "Agent = Model + Harness." — Industry shorthand (multiple sources)

> "Today, AI agents can autonomously do coding tasks that take humans over fourteen hours." — METR

> "67% of [Devin's] PRs are now merged vs 34% last year." — Cognition Devin 2025 review

> "TELUS saving 500,000 engineering hours and accelerating shipping times by 30%." — Anthropic Agentic Coding Trends 2026

> "OpenAI's team built and shipped a production beta product containing roughly one million lines of code — with zero lines written by human hands during a five-month internal experiment." — OpenAI Harness Engineering

> "67% of PRs are now merged" — Devin

> "Goldman Sachs piloted the autonomous coding agent alongside their 12,000 human developers" — Devin annual review

> Mario Zechner: "AI coding agents are producing 'garbage' — and the industry needs to slow down." — paraphrased on Pragmatic Engineer / BigGo
