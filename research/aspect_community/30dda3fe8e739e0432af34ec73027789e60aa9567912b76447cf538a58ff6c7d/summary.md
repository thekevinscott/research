# Summary

AB's Reflections grounds the dark factory framing in concrete metrics: Cursor's internal 35% agent-generated PR rate, three-person teams replacing twenty-person ones, and rapidly-falling compute costs creating asymmetric advantages. It frames the pattern less as "no humans" and more as "humans relocate to architecture and outcomes," softening the Shapiro/StrongDM stricture and making the pattern feel achievable for non-frontier teams.

Sentiment is optimistic-pragmatist. The piece argues that the structural opportunity — flatter, smaller, faster teams — outweighs the technical and governance risks for most organisations willing to invest in validation infrastructure. It is one of the more-shared mid-2026 pieces among startup-side engineering leaders.
