# Summary

Andrew Baker's March 2026 essay is the strongest "the gap is real and accelerating" piece in the discourse. He argues that the industry is splitting into dark factories and everyone else, with the laggards measurably slower despite using AI tools. His list of three blockers — validation environment, liability, and competitive moat erosion — has become the standard framework for engineering-leadership discussions of the pattern.

Sentiment from Baker is alarmist but not dystopian: he treats Level-5 adoption as both inevitable and selectively distributed, with security tooling, internal tools, and migrations leading. Sceptics in the comment section push back on whether "three teams" is enough to declare a trend; Baker's reply is that the existence proof matters more than the headcount.
