# Summary

The Cow-Shed piece extrapolates the dark factory framework into audit, banking, and legal work, treating software as the leading indicator and arguing each profession will follow the same Five Levels arc. Audit and routine banking work are framed as well-suited because they are regulated and deterministic-testable; novel legal drafting is framed as poorly suited because acceptance is interpretive.

Sentiment is bullish on broad applicability but caveats heavily on the validation-loop quality required. It is the most-cited piece linking dark factories to non-software white-collar work and is widely shared in audit-firm and big-law innovation circles.
