Reported case studies and metrics:

> "In early 2026, pioneering organizations demonstrated that as few as three engineers can run a software factory where humans no longer write code."

> "BCG Platinion also reported their own results: A five-day AI task force converted two business-critical enterprise applications, initially estimated at hundreds of person-days — 20% productivity gains per application were achieved after just two days."

Strategic claim:

> "Autonomous AI agents build, test, and ship software solutions around the clock. Humans define business intent. Rather than eliminating humans entirely, the relocation of human effort is critical."

> "The quality of what the factory delivers depends on two things: how well the factory itself is architected (its processes, harnesses, and stage gates) and how precisely an organization can articulate what it wants."

## Summary (2 paragraphs)

BCG Platinion's "Dark Software Factory" report (March 2026) is the most cited consultancy-side framing of the pattern. It defines the dark factory as an autonomous software delivery pipeline, holds up Spotify (650 AI PRs/month, no human-written code since December 2025) and StrongDM (three engineers, no human code) as case studies, and reports BCG's own five-day enterprise application conversion as a productivity proof point.

The report's strategic argument — that quality depends jointly on how the factory is architected and how precisely the organisation can articulate intent — is now the standard executive framing. It moved the conversation from "can this work?" to "where do we deploy it?" in C-suite circles, and is the principal channel by which the pattern reached non-technical leadership.
