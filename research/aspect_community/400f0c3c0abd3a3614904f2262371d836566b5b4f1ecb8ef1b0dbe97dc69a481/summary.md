# Summary

The Pragmatic CTO newsletter reframes the dark factory as a CFO-grade decision rather than a technology choice. Its headline numbers — token spend per "engineer" running $400-600k/year, exceeding median dev salary, against AI-generated code carrying 1.5-2x more security vulnerabilities than human-written code — are widely quoted by sceptics and feature in management decks across the industry.

Editorially, the post is firmly Level-3/Level-4 pragmatist: the dark factory works for StrongDM-shaped teams (security tooling, reliability culture, deterministic output, mature domain) but is irrational for most companies in 2026. This is one of the most-cited "yes, but" pieces in the discourse.
