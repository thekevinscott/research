# Hacker News: "The Five Levels: From spicy autocomplete to the dark factory"

**Posted:** Late January 2026 (linking Dan Shapiro's January 23, 2026 blog post; revisited heavily after Simon Willison's Feb 7 2026 follow-up)

## Reconstructed thread context (from search snippets)

The submission links Dan Shapiro's "The Five Levels: from Spicy Autocomplete to the Dark Factory," which adapted SAE driving-automation levels to AI-assisted coding. The post defines:

- Level 0 — "Spicy autocomplete," a.k.a. original GitHub Copilot or pasting from ChatGPT.
- Level 1 — Coding intern: boilerplate with full human review.
- Level 2 — Junior dev / pair programmer.
- Level 3 — "The developer" — AI writes most code, human is full-time reviewer.
- Level 4 — Engineering team: collaborate on specs, agents do the work.
- Level 5 — "The dark software factory ... like a factory run by robots where the lights are out because robots don't need to see."

## Summary (2 paragraphs)

This Hacker News thread is the original watering-hole for the "Five Levels" framework that gave the dark factory its English name. Comments split between skeptical engineers ("a Dark Factory is a lot more work than the model, and often perpendicular to the actual product") and a smaller group with first-hand exposure ("I've talked to a team that's doing the dark factory pattern hinted at here. It works."). The thread is notably sober about Level 5's cost: building digital twins, harnesses, and validators is presented as more labour-intensive than writing the underlying product manually.

Sentiment-wise, the thread anchors the early-2026 community framing of the pattern: a real but narrow capability frontier, gated on validation infrastructure and applicable mostly to teams with deep reliability-engineering experience. It is the canonical HN entry point that subsequent StrongDM and Simon Willison threads (HN 46924711 / 46924426) repeatedly cite back to.
