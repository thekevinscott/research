# Overview

Simon Willison's Feb 7, 2026 post is the canonical introduction of the "Dark Factory" pattern to mainstream developer discourse. He describes a visit to StrongDM's three-person AI team (Justin McCarthy, Jay Taylor, Navan Chauhan), who built — in three months — a coding-agent harness, a "Digital Twin Universe" of behavioral clones of third-party SaaS APIs, and a swarm of simulated agents that exercise scenarios. The team's three cardinal rules are: code must not be written by humans, code must not be reviewed by humans, and if you are not spending at least $1,000/day per engineer on tokens, your factory has room to grow.

The validation contribution of the piece is the introduction of "scenarios" as out-of-codebase user stories used as a holdout-style test set, validated flexibly by LLM judges rather than by brittle assertion-based unit tests. Combined with high-fidelity service twins (Okta, Jira, Slack, Google Workspace) and a feedback loop, this lets a swarm of agents run end-to-end behaviors against a virtual world without any human reading the produced code. The post is the seed reference for nearly all subsequent "dark factory" / "harness-first" / "scenario testing" discourse in 2026.
