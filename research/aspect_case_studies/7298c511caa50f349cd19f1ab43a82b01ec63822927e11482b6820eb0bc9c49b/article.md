## What this is

A first-hand month-long case study where the author scaled autonomous coding agents to handle production-grade engineering. The experiment evolved into a permanent production methodology and shipped a real product (production log analytics backend).

## Headline metric

- 18,000 commits in 30 days from a "factory" of agents.

## Direct quotes / near-quotes from snippets

> The experiment "scaled autonomous agents to handle production-grade software engineering workloads."

> "The resulting code exhibits a level of consistency, standardization, and traceability that is counterintuitively superior to pure human-driven development."

> "Ultimately, they shipped the backend for production log analytics using the methods discussed in the article."

## Why it matters as a case study

- This is one of the earliest "I ran a dark factory for a month" reports - it predates the StrongDM manifesto by several months and validated the pattern independently.
- The author claims AI-factory output is more consistent, more standardized, and more traceable than human output - a non-obvious claim that contradicts the common assumption that unsupervised AI code is messier.
- It produced a real shipped product, not a demo: a production log analytics backend.

## Takeaway

A pre-StrongDM, indie-published proof point that scaling AI agents into a "factory" can both ship real software and improve consistency/traceability metrics versus human teams.
