# Summary

Ona ran a 10-day dark factory experiment and published the numbers: 375 PRs, 67k lines of code, 0 human-written code. The team's central finding is that outcome quality is overwhelmingly determined by spec quality, not by model capability - "most bugs were not capability failures but specification failures." When they invested in a detailed product spec on Day 3, the factory produced a working app complete with authentication, workspaces, a rich text editor, search and invites.

The other big lesson is that the factory has no inherent sense of what users need next, so they bolted on a feedback widget and a digest automation that turned user feedback into specification updates. They also flag a new failure mode peculiar to dark factories: not under-shipping but over-shipping. "The real risk of software factories isn't that teams ship too little, but that they ship too much."
