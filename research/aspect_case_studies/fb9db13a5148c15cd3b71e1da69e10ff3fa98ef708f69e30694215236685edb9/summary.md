# Summary

Nate Jones's Substack essay is one of the most-cited operationalizations of the dark factory pattern. He frames the StrongDM case ("three engineers... run a software factory where no human writes code and no human reviews code") as the concrete realization of Level 5 in Dan Shapiro's five-level framework, and contrasts it with empirical data showing that most developers using AI tools are actually 19% slower in a randomized control trial - while believing they are 20-24% faster.

The post argues the real bottleneck is no longer coding speed but organisational design and specification quality: frontier teams have rebuilt their workflows so the bottleneck moved from "how fast you can write code" to "how precisely you can describe what should exist." It includes "5 prompts to get from level 2 to 5" aimed at helping teams stuck at level 2 climb the ladder, and is widely linked from other dark-factory writeups.
