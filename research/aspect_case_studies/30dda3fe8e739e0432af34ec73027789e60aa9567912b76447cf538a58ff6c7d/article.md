## Framing (paraphrase)

The post says some companies are already running "dark factories" - engineering organisations where AI agents do most of the work and humans orchestrate. The author treats StrongDM as the canonical reference but writes from the perspective of an engineering manager observing the trend.

## Direct quotes / near-quotes from snippets

> "This isn't about replacing engineers - it's about fundamentally changing what engineers do."

> The discipline moves from "write good code" to "design good systems for code to be written in."

> "Dark factories aren't ungoverned. They're heavily governed in a different way."

> "Linters, formatters, comprehensive test suites, design pattern enforcement - these become pre-conditions rather than suggestions. Agents are configured to seek completion only when all guardrails pass."

> "A three-person team with agents can produce output that previously required twenty people. The cost of compute is a fraction of equivalent human labour and falling rapidly."

## Key claims

- Engineering work moves up the abstraction stack: from syntax/implementation to constraint design, validation, and feedback-loop engineering.
- Code review shifts from line-by-line human inspection to AI review with human spot-checks on critical paths.
- Capital efficiency becomes "native" - small teams with agent fleets reach output previously requiring much larger teams.
- Linters/formatters/tests become hard pre-conditions baked into the agent loop, not soft suggestions.

## Takeaway

The author treats StrongDM as proof-of-concept and argues the org-design implication is that the role of "senior engineer" reshapes around system/constraint design rather than coding craft.
