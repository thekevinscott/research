# Summary

AB's Reflections argues that "dark factory" engineering orgs - where AI agents do most of the writing and humans orchestrate - are no longer hypothetical. Citing StrongDM as the canonical case, the author claims the discipline of software engineering shifts from "write good code" to "design good systems for code to be written in," with humans focused on constraints, validation, and feedback loops rather than syntax.

The post pushes back on the misconception that dark factories are ungoverned. They are heavily governed - just differently. Linters, formatters, comprehensive test suites and design-pattern enforcement become hard pre-conditions baked into the agent loop, and code review becomes AI-led with human spot-checks on critical paths. The economic punchline: a three-person team plus agents can match output that used to need twenty people, with compute cost a fraction of equivalent human labor.
