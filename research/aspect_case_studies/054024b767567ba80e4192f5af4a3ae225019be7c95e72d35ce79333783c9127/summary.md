# Summary

Simon Willison's February 7, 2026 post is the canonical first-hand introduction of the Dark Factory pattern to the broader AI coding community. He visited the StrongDM AI team - Justin McCarthy, Jay Taylor and Navan Chauhan - in October 2025, three months after the team was founded, and saw working demos of their coding-agent harness, their Digital Twin Universe (clones of Okta, Jira, Slack, Google Docs/Drive/Sheets), and their satisfaction-testing framework with simulated test agents running through scenarios.

He calls it "the most ambitious form of AI-assisted software development I've seen yet" and highlights two cardinal rules - "Code must not be written by humans" and "Code must not be reviewed by humans" - plus the McCarthy benchmark that "if you haven't spent at least \$1,000 on tokens today per human engineer, your software factory has room for improvement." The post emphasises the irony that StrongDM's product is security software for managing user permissions across SaaS - which Willison flags as "the last thing you would expect to be built using unreviewed LLM code." Almost every other dark-factory writeup is a direct response to this post.
