## Reconstructed from snippets

The most-circulated consulting / enterprise framing of the Dark Factory pattern.

### Quoted thesis

> "In early 2026, pioneering organizations demonstrated that as few as three engineers can run a software factory where humans no longer write code. Autonomous AI agents build, test, and ship software solutions around the clock."

### Headline case

> "Engineers at Spotify have not written a single line of code since December 2025, with the company merging 650 AI-generated pull requests per month, cutting the time required for large-scale migrations by 90%."

### BCG's own pilot

> "BCG Platinion delivered proof of value in a large-scale legacy migration where a five-day AI task force converted two business-critical enterprise applications with 20% productivity gains per application achieved after just two days, with project productivity gains exceeding 50% at scale."

### The two competencies (quoted)

> "Two competencies are decisive: harness engineering, the discipline of designing, building, and refining the factory while constantly feeding information to its assembly lines, and intent thinking, the ability to translate business needs."

### Strategic implication

> "Legacy modernization programs shelved because of prohibitive costs and multi-year timelines are now viable, allowing organizations to finally shed their legacy burden and redirect spending toward innovation."

## Two-paragraph summary

BCG Platinion's "The Dark Software Factory" insight piece (with a companion March 2026 PDF) is the consulting industry's framing of the pattern for enterprise audiences. It frames the trend as decisive — three-engineer teams running 24/7 software factories — and supplies the most-quoted enterprise data point: Spotify engineers reportedly haven't written code since December 2025, merging 650 AI-generated PRs per month and cutting large-scale migration time by 90%. BCG's own five-day legacy-migration pilot is offered as proof of value.

The strategic argument names two new core competencies — harness engineering and intent thinking — borrowing OpenAI's vocabulary directly. The piece is the bridge between technical writeups (Willison, StrongDM, OpenAI, Anthropic) and CIO/CTO purchasing conversations, and is the engine driving 2026 enterprise interest in autonomous coding pipelines.
