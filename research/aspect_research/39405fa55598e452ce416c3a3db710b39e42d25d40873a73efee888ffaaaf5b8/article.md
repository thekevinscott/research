## Two-paragraph summary

LangChain's agentic engineering post argues that the natural evolution of coding agents is toward loosely coupled, multi-agent engineering teams that operate across developer and team boundaries — moving from task-level execution to system-level collaboration. The post positions LangGraph (orchestration) and LangSmith (observability/evals) as the runtime substrate, and constructs the conceptual bridge between today's bounded-loop assistants and tomorrow's Dark-Factory-style autonomous teams.

The companion April 2026 "State of Agent Engineering" report supplies the demand-side data: 57% of surveyed organizations have agents in production and a further 30% are actively building toward deployment. Together they form the framework-vendor case for the trend Anthropic and OpenAI document from the model side. Highly relevant (3).
