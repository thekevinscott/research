# METR — Time Horizon 1.1 (January 29, 2026)

URL: https://metr.org/blog/2026-1-29-time-horizon-1-1/

## Reconstructed from snippets

METR's January 29, 2026 update to its task-completion-time-horizon benchmark — the most-cited external metric for "how long can an autonomous agent work without humans?"

### Quoted methodology

> "The task-completion time horizon is the task duration (measured by human expert completion time) at which an AI agent is predicted to succeed with a given level of reliability. The 50%-time horizon is the duration at which an agent is predicted to succeed half the time."

> "They estimate the duration it takes a human expert to complete each task, fit a logistic curve to predict the probability an AI agent successfully completes tasks as a function of human task duration, and find the task duration where the fitted curve intersects with 50% (or 80%) success probability."

### Updates in v1.1

> "The suite was increased from 170 to 228 tasks. They added 73 tasks, removed 15 tasks, and updated 53 tasks. Notably, the number of long tasks (estimated to take humans 8 or more hours) increased from 14 to 31."

> "They moved their evaluation infrastructure from Vivaria to Inspect, an open-source framework for AI evaluations developed by the UK AI Security Institute."

### Current frontier numbers (early 2026, snippet)

> "Claude Opus 4.6 leads at ~14.5 hours, followed by GPT-5.2 at ~6.6 hours and Claude Opus 4.5 at ~4.8 hours."

### The doubling-time finding (companion paper, arXiv 2503.14499)

> "[The 50% time horizon] has been consistently exponentially increasing over the past 6 years, with a doubling time of around 7 months. However, in 2024-2025, time horizons doubled every 4 months, down from every 7 months over 2019-2025."
