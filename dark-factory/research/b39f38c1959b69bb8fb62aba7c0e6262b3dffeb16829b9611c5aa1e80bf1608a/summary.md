Simon Willison's May 2025 post 'Two publishers and three authors fail to understand what vibe coding means' draws a sharp line between Karpathy's original definition (forget the code exists, throwaway projects) and what he sees marketers and book authors describing (any LLM-assisted coding). Willison's bright-line rule: he won't commit code to his repository if he can't explain to a teammate exactly what it does. Vibe-coding-as-Karpathy-defined-it is fine for prototypes; vibe-coding-your-way-to-production is irresponsible.

For Dark Factory failure analysis, this is the canonical pre-Dark-Factory framing from the writer who later coined the 'Dark Factory' label for Shapiro's Level 5. Willison's own position is that Dark Factory is 'basically unproven' and that he 'hasn't been brave enough to try it' on his own projects (per HN comments three months later). The distinction matters because Dark Factory advocates frequently cite Willison as a sympathizer, while his actual position is closer to 'interesting demo, do not ship'.
