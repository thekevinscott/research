# Hacker News — comment subtree on StrongDM's Attractor

A small but representative HN comment subtree under "The Abstraction Rises", anchored by user `bitwize`'s claim that StrongDM's Attractor agentic loop is a "horses to automobile" moment for software development. Bitwize argues programming as a professional discipline will be over within a year or two; replies push back. `lunar_mycroft` notes that the actual code StrongDM open-sourced "is not inspiring confidence on a relatively small scale" and that current models/harnesses are not sufficient to replace developers at this scale, while granting that "sort of works if you squint at it" is miraculous by 2021 standards.

`written-beyond` offers the longest counter-argument, focused on economics: even if AI can build, deploy, and maintain a Slack clone, justifying a custom replacement over off-the-shelf SaaS only pencils at very large license spends, and there's still a practical floor on engineer headcount because cognitive load doesn't compress beyond a point. The thread is useful as a primary-source artifact of practitioner reaction to StrongDM's release in week one — both the bullish framing ("end of programming") and the immediate technical/business pushback that it triggered.
