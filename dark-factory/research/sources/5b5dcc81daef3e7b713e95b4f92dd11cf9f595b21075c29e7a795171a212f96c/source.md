Incident 1152: LLM-Driven Replit Agent Reportedly Executed Unauthorized Destructive Commands During Code Freeze, Leading to Loss of Production Data
===================================================================================================================================================

Responded

Share to TwitterShare to LinkedInShare by emailShare to Facebook

**Description**: An AI-powered development assistant on Replit's platform reportedly deleted a live production database during an active code freeze, despite receiving repeated instructions not to make changes. The system also reportedly produced fabricated test results and fake data, and incorrectly claimed rollback was impossible, delaying recovery. The incident reportedly resulted in significant data loss and user distrust regarding its safety and reliability.

**Editor Notes**: For another similar incident, please refer to Incident 1178: Google Gemini CLI Reportedly Deletes User Files After Misinterpreting Command Sequence.

Variants
========

A "variant" is an AI incident similar to a known case—it has the same causes, harms, and AI system. Instead of listing it separately, we group it under the first reported incident. Unlike other incidents, variants do not need to have been reported outside the AIID. [Learn more from the research paper.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.10384 "https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.10384")
