# StrongDM Software Factory — Story (Justin McCarthy, Feb 6 2026)

The canonical primary-source landing page for StrongDM's "Software Factory" — the most-cited Level-5 / Dark-Factory case study to date. Co-founder/CTO Justin McCarthy describes how a three-person AI team formed in July 2025 (himself, Jay Taylor, Navan Chauhan) and committed up front to two charter rules: "Code must not be written by humans" and "Code must not be reviewed by humans." McCarthy frames the late-2024 Claude 3.5 v2 release plus Cursor's YOLO mode as the inflection that made "non-interactive" or "grown" software viable, because long-horizon agentic loops finally began compounding correctness rather than error. He offers a concrete benchmark for adoption: per-engineer-day token spend below ~$1,000 means the factory has room to grow.

The post lays out the conceptual stack the rest of the site fleshes out. Hand-written tests fail in this regime (agents reward-hack `return true`), so the team replaced "test" with **scenario** — end-to-end natural-language user stories stored as a holdout set outside the codebase, evaluated by an LLM-as-judge — and replaced boolean pass/fail with a probabilistic **satisfaction** score over observed trajectories. To run scenarios at volume against integrations the codebase touches, they built the **Digital Twin Universe**: behavioral clones of Okta, Jira, Slack, Google Docs, Drive, and Sheets that respond like the real APIs but allow thousands of scenarios per hour without rate limits, abuse detection, or API costs. McCarthy positions the DTU as evidence of an "Agentic Moment" economics shift: things engineers always wanted but self-censored away as too expensive (full SaaS replicas) are now routine.
