# BCG Platinion — The Dark Software Factory

BCG Platinion frames the "Dark Software Factory" as a 2026 inflection: AI agents that build, test, and ship software around the clock while humans only define intent and review outcomes. Reported productivity gains are 3–5x on average, with case-study evidence from Spotify (650 AI-generated PRs/month, no human-written code since December 2025), OpenAI (a million-line product in five months by three engineers), and BCG Platinion's own legacy migrations (50%+ project gains).

The piece argues two competencies decide who wins: harness engineering (designing the factory's processes, encoding standards into agent instructions) and intent thinking (translating business needs into precise testable specs). It positions Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, and Antigravity as the agent runtimes and recommends building "five pillars" — harness, intent, governance, autonomy, and review — rather than treating Dark Factory as a single product.
