# StrongDM Blog — The StrongDM Software Factory: Building Software with AI

The official StrongDM corporate-blog announcement that mirrors and contextualizes the factory.strongdm.ai site for a wider audience. It introduces the Software Factory to StrongDM's customer base, frames the two charter rules (no human-written code, no human-reviewed code) for executive readers, and connects the engineering practice to StrongDM's product portfolio in identity and access management. The post is written for buyers and prospective customers more than for practitioners — but it is a primary-source statement from the company, not third-party commentary.

It also makes claims about scale and outcomes: throughput, lines-of-code shipped, and the role of the DTU and Attractor in their daily engineering workflow. As a customer-facing artifact it underscores StrongDM's strategic bet — that the factory pattern is not just internal R&D but the way they intend to build product going forward. For the cases aspect this is useful as the corporate-marketing-tier framing of the same case the technical site documents in depth, and as a reference point for how the Dark Factory pattern is being sold up the org chart in B2B SaaS in 2026.
