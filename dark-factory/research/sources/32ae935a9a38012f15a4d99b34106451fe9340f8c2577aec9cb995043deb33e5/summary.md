Wiz Research disclosed (July 2025) a critical authentication bypass in Base44, a vibe-coding platform recently acquired by Wix. The flaw allowed any attacker to register an account against any customer's private application by knowing only its app_id (a non-secret value visible in URLs). Once registered, the attacker had full access to private apps generated by Base44 customers — including ones built by enterprises. Wiz coordinated disclosure; Base44 patched within 24 hours.

For Dark Factory failure analysis, Base44 is a 'platform-level' failure: the underlying agent produced apps with sensible-looking auth, but the platform itself routed registration through a broken endpoint. This illustrates a category Dark Factory advocates often skip: the agent's output may be locally correct, but the substrate the agent runs on is not held to the same scrutiny. When humans are not in the review loop, neither agent nor platform errors are caught before exposure to real users.
