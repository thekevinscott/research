Sean Goedecke (engineer at GitHub, where Copilot ships) calls METR's study 'really good' — rare praise from someone whose employer is a primary AI-coding-tool vendor. He works through the methodology, accepts the headline 19% slowdown, and offers a reconciliation: AI tools are best in settings where the developer doesn't already know the codebase deeply, and worst in settings where they do. Experienced devs working on their own repos are the worst case, and that's exactly what METR measured.

For Dark Factory failure analysis, Goedecke's analysis is useful because it frames the slowdown as a deployment-context problem rather than a 'AI coding doesn't work' problem. The implication: Dark Factory deployment that puts agents into deep, evolving codebases — exactly the StrongDM case — may face the exact friction METR documented, except without the human in the loop to course-correct. Whether agents can navigate codebase context well enough to break the pattern is, per Goedecke, unproven.
