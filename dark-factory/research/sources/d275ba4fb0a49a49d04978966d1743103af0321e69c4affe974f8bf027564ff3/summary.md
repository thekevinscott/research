Arize (an AI observability vendor with production telemetry from agent deployments) publishes a field analysis of common production failures across customer agent deployments. Their taxonomy includes: silent reasoning errors (agent confidently produces wrong output), tool-call hallucinations (invokes nonexistent APIs or misuses real ones), runaway loops (agent retries the same broken step until budget exhausted), context-window collapse (agent loses track of multi-step task), and false-success reporting (agent claims completion without verification).

For Dark Factory failure analysis, Arize's data is the closest thing to a population-level survey of how unattended agents actually fail in production. The catalog supports a key skeptic claim: the failure modes are not edge cases, they are the modal experience of running agents at scale without human review. Dark Factory designs that don't explicitly engineer against each of these categories will hit them. Arize argues for behavioral-baseline regression testing as mitigation.
