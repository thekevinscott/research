Abi Noda (DX) — whose firm builds developer-productivity tooling for large engineering orgs — writes a careful walkthrough of METR's methodology and limitations. Noda's framing is that METR's result is plausible and important, even though it is one study with 16 developers. He highlights the perception/reality gap as the most significant finding: developers consistently believed they were faster with AI even when measurement showed they were slower. This has direct implications for how engineering orgs should evaluate AI-tool ROI.

For Dark Factory failure analysis, the DX summary is the pragmatic 'eng-leader-facing' version of the METR result. Its key contribution to the failure catalog: agentic coding pipelines that self-report progress through the agent's own assessments are at risk of the same perception/reality inversion METR found in humans. Without external measurement (independent harnesses, shipped-feature counts, defect rates), Dark Factory operators cannot tell whether they are actually faster.
