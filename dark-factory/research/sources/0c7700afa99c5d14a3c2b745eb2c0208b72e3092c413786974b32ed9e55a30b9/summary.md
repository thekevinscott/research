Becker et al.'s arXiv preprint (2507.09089) is the formal write-up of METR's randomized controlled trial measuring whether AI coding tools accelerate experienced open-source developers. The headline finding: AI-allowed conditions take 19% longer than AI-disallowed conditions, with a confidence interval that excludes any speedup. This sits in tension with self-report data and with industry benchmark hype.

For Dark Factory failure analysis, the paper is load-bearing because it is one of the only RCTs in the literature that measures real-world coding throughput rather than synthetic benchmarks. The authors stress that the result is contextual — it does not generalize to all developers or settings — but it directly undercuts the assumption that agent-driven coding is automatically faster. Pre-2024 claim ('AI makes coding faster') no longer survives without empirical support.
