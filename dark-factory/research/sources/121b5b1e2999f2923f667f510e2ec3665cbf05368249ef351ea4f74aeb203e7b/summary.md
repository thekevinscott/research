Pulumi's May 2026 blog post argues that the Dark Factory pattern (Shapiro Level 5, no human review) is most consequential when applied to infrastructure-as-code rather than application code, because IaC carries irreversible blast-radius (provisioning, secrets, IAM). They sketch a four-phase rollout: improve specs, then add isolated holdout-scenario validation, then autonomous merge gated at 90% scenario pass, finally lights-out. The piece treats StrongDM (32k LOC, security software) and Stripe Minions (1000+ PRs/week) as the anchor success cases.

Most teams are at levels 2–3; only experimental teams are at Level 5 for infra. The post stresses an isolation wall — the same model that writes infra cannot validate it — and recommends Pulumi CrossGuard as a policy gate. The implicit claim is that infra is harder to dark-factory than app code but the payoff is larger because human review of infra diffs is already lossy.
