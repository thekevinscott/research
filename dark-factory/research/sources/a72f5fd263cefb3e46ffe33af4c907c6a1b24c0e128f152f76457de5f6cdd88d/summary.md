Specode (March 2026) catalogs concrete failure modes of vibe-coded / agent-coded apps under HIPAA. LLM-generated healthcare code routinely stores PHI in plaintext, omits AES-256 at rest and TLS 1.2+ in transit unless explicitly prompted, and skips audit logging entirely — a direct HIPAA violation. The most striking failure mode is hallucinated security: an `encryptPHI()` function that's actually base64, JWTs without expiry, no role-based access.

Real scenarios cited include a mental-health chatbot logging therapy details in plaintext and analytics SDKs capturing lab results without authorization. The argument is that healthcare is the clearest domain where Dark Factory autonomy fails by default — compliance is invisible to scenario tests unless deliberately encoded — and that 60–80% of any agent-driven time savings has to be reinvested in security hardening to be net safe.
