# StrongDM Software Factory — Digital Twin Universe (DTU)

A primary-source deep-dive on StrongDM's Digital Twin Universe — the technique that, more than any other, makes their Dark Factory tractable. The DTU consists of behavioral clones of third-party services the software depends on: Okta, Jira, Slack, Google Docs, Drive, and Sheets, with replicas of their APIs, edge cases, and observable behaviors. The page details why production systems can't be the validation target — rate limits, abuse detection, API cost, irreversibility — and what the DTU enables instead: thousands of scenarios per hour, deterministic replay, fault injection, and adversarial conditions that would be dangerous against live services.

The page is also the clearest articulation of why this works only now. Building a high-fidelity SaaS replica was always technically possible but economically prohibited — a "deliberate naivete" is required of factory operators to ignore the old self-censoring constraints of Software 1.0. StrongDM's claim is empirical, not theoretical: they did the build, they validate against it daily, and the cost-per-scenario is a tiny fraction of running against real integrations. The DTU is presented as proof that token economics now favor approaches that engineers previously knew about but never proposed.
