InfoQ's coverage of the METR study is notable for contextualizing the 19% slowdown against industry productivity claims (e.g., GitHub Copilot's 55% speedup figure from a 2022 lab study). InfoQ notes that the METR setting — experienced devs, mature repos, complex issues — is closer to 'real engineering work' than the synthetic 'add a feature to a fresh app' tasks that drive vendor speedup numbers. This positions METR as the realism corrective to lab-bench claims.

For Dark Factory failure analysis, the InfoQ piece is useful as a tier-3 synthesis that links METR to the broader 'is AI coding actually faster?' debate. It also surfaces a key disagreement: developer self-report (faster) versus measured time (slower). If Dark Factory operators are calibrating success on agent self-report (e.g., 'task completed', 'satisfaction score'), they are using the same metric class that mis-led METR's human subjects.
