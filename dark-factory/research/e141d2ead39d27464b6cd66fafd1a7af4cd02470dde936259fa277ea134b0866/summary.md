Sun & Staron (Chalmers / Software Center, January 2026) interview ten senior engineers across four embedded-software firms (communications, electronics, energy control, machinery). The core finding is that Dark-Factory-style autonomy is currently incompatible with embedded for three reasons: (1) LLM non-determinism breaks the reproducibility that embedded verification depends on; (2) no certification path exists for agent-generated code under DO-178C / IEC 61508 regimes; (3) embedded systems have 10+ year traceability requirements and you cannot archive an LLM the way you archive a compiler.

They propose a risk-tiered autonomy model where docs, test generation, and non-production builds can move toward lights-out but safety-critical code stays human-in-the-loop. The paper's signature line — 'we can check in the compiler from ten years ago, but not the LLM' — captures why Dark Factory does not generalize to embedded today.
