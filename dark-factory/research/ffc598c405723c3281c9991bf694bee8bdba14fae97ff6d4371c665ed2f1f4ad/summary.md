The Next Web details Lovable's December-2025 security crisis: a vibe-coding platform valued at $6.6B with eight million users, where researchers found that Lovable apps consistently shipped with broken auth/RLS — and the company appeared to systematically close inbound bug reports without addressing them. One Lovable-built application examined by researchers contained 16 exploitable vulnerabilities; another 48-day window saw mass exposure of customer source code, database credentials, AI chat histories, and PII.

For Dark Factory failure analysis, Lovable is the most-cited 'commodity vibe-coding-at-scale' failure: the platform's agents produce apps fast, the apps go to real users, and nobody — agent, human, or platform — does the security review that would have flagged the structural Firebase/RLS misconfigurations. The crisis falsifies the implicit Dark Factory premise that agents have absorbed enough security-best-practice training to ship default-safe code.
